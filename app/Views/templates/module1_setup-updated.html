Teacher Tip
Students should use the questions to guide a presentation on Rube Goldberg and his machines. You might want to develop additional guidelines and requirements and/or a rubric for presentation purposes. You can decide if this will be more research focused with an informal presentation, or if you would prefer to do a formal presentation with accompanying media, rubrics, etc.

Possible Outcomes
Ruben Lucius Goldberg (1883 - 1970) was a cartoonist, technology lover, and writer. He grew up in San Francisco and had many jobs including designing sewers, sportswriter, cartoonist, and writing editorials. He worked for many newspapers including the San Francisco Bulletin, The New York Sun, and the New York Evening Mail. Goldberg won a Pulitzer Prize for a cartoon called “Peace Today” in 1938 which pictures a house on top of an atomic bomb about to fall off of a cliff. Many of his cartoons feature a character named Professor Lucifer Gorgonzola Butts who creates many strange and complicated machines.
